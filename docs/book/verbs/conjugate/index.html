Verb and Adjective Conjugates

The most complicated form to put verbs and adjectives into is the conjugate form. The conjugate form starts off by changing the verb or adjective into the stem form. Once in its stem form, how to change it depends on the last remaining syllable block in the word. There are 9 different rules for making the conjugate form, and they all depend on the last vowel and syllable block in a word’s stem form.
